The Colosseum

The Colosseum, an iconic symbol of ancient Rome, was the largest amphitheater ever built. It hosted gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and other public spectacles.

The Roman Forum

The Roman Forum was the center of political, commercial, and judicial life in ancient Rome. It housed important government buildings, temples, and monuments.

The Pantheon

The Pantheon is one of the best-preserved ancient Roman buildings. Originally a temple dedicated to all the gods, it was later converted into a church.

The Baths of Caracalla

The Baths of Caracalla were one of the largest and most impressive public bath complexes in ancient Rome, featuring elaborate mosaics and sculptures.

The Arch of Constantine

The Arch of Constantine is a triumphal arch dedicated to Emperor Constantine the Great. It commemorates his victory at the Battle of Milvian Bridge in 312 AD.

The Circus Maximus

The Circus Maximus was the largest stadium in ancient Rome, primarily used for chariot racing. It could accommodate up to 150,000 spectators.
